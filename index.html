Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

When riding a bike, prioritize safety: always wear a helmet, obey traffic rules, and stay visible. Practice balance and braking techniques, especially on hills or in wet conditions. Check your bike’s condition before each ride—brakes, tires, and chain are essential. Use hand signals for turns, keep a safe distance from vehicles, and stay alert to your surroundings. Regular maintenance ensures a smoother, safer ride.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Check your helmet’s age—most need replacement every 5–7 years due to material degradation. Look for signs like cracks, faded color, frayed straps, or a loose fit. If it’s been in an accident or dropped, replace it immediately, as impact can weaken protection. Some helmets also have a label with the manufacture date to help determine when to replace it.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding a bike that’s been in storage, inspect it thoroughly. Check the tires for cracks or flat spots, ensure the brakes work smoothly, and test the battery if applicable. Look for any rust, especially on the chain and gears, and ensure the lights and indicators function properly. It’s also wise to change the oil and consider a professional tune-up for safety.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Follow these key rules when riding: always wear a helmet and visible clothing, obey traffic laws, and use hand signals for turns. Stay in bike lanes when available, keep a safe distance from vehicles, and avoid distractions like headphones. Regularly check your bike’s brakes, tires, and lights for safety. Be mindful of pedestrians, and ride predictably to ensure others on the road can anticipate your moves.
